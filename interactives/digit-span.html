Why backward is harder

Forward digit span can be done partly by short-term storage: hold the digits and repeat them. Backward digit span adds a manipulation step: hold the digits, reorder them, and then respond. That is why it is a better demonstration of working memory.

Storage demand:
Keep the digits active long enough to answer.
Manipulation demand:
Reverse the order before responding.
Psychology point: studying often requires backward-digit-span-like work. You must hold ideas in mind, compare them, reorder them, connect them, and apply them. Looking at information is not enough; learning requires mental manipulation.
